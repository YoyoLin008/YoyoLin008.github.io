Highlights from the log

Quick notes from the logbook that capture why each segment mattered, beyond the miles flown.

First-time routes always feel like opening a new chapter. I jot down the airport codes, aircraft type, and any quirks—like the tiny regional jet that felt more like a bus in the sky or the surprise upgrade that turned a red-eye into a quiet retreat.

Window seats are where the best details live. Cloud textures, wing flex during turbulence, and city grids sparkling at night all make it into the log. Even short hops become memorable when I connect the view to the ground stories below.

I also note the cabins and crews that stand out. A thoughtful announcement or a well-designed seatback can change the entire vibe of a flight. Recording those little touches keeps the log human, not just a spreadsheet of destinations.
