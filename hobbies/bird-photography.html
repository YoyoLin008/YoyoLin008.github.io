Field notes

Memorable sightings

A few written snapshots from recent trips and early-morning walks when the light and the birds lined up just right.

Coastal marshes are where I learn patience. Watching egrets fish in the shallows teaches me to anticipate movement and respect slow moments. The muted palette of water and reeds makes every bright wingbeat stand out.

Urban parks surprise me the most. Between joggers and dog walkers, there is always a flash of color—parakeets in tall trees, sparrows pilfering crumbs, or a hawk circling above skyscrapers. It reminds me that wildlife adapts faster than we notice.

Traveling for migration seasons is my favorite ritual. I keep a notebook of dates, weather, and locations so I can revisit the same spots and compare how flocks change year to year. Those notes keep the stories alive even without the camera in hand.
