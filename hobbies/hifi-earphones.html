Setups & impressions

Chain experiments

Notes on how different rigs change the music—no gear photos needed, just the impressions that stuck.

The desk stack is where I test nuance. A neutral DAC feeding a tube amp smooths harsh recordings while keeping cymbal sparkle intact. I keep track of which IEMs balance out the warmth so the midrange never feels muddy.

Portable pairings are all about convenience without losing character. Dongle DACs with compact IEMs make it easy to hop between campus and flights, and I jot down how each combo handles busy arrangements or hissy mixes.

Weekend listening leans into comfort. I reach for relaxed tunings, throw on jazz trios or math rock, and note which amps make bass lines feel textured instead of bloated. Those impressions guide the next round of swaps.
